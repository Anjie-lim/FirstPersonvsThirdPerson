POV Classification Quiz: Character Perspective

Instructions: For each scenario, determine if the gameplay qualifies as First-Person or Third-Person based on the camera’s relationship to the character.

1. Dungeon Exploration

You are exploring a dungeon. You can see your character's hands holding a torch in front of you, but you cannot see your own head or back.

2. Forest Run

You are running through a forest. The camera is positioned behind the character, and you can see their entire body and backpack as they move.

3. Looking Around the Room

You are standing in a room. When you move the mouse to look around, the camera stays inside the head of the character, showing only what they would see.

4. Strategy Game View

You are playing a strategy game where you click on the ground to move a character. The camera is high in the sky looking down at the character from an angle.

5. Emote Camera Pullback

You press a button to “Emote” like waving or dancing. The camera suddenly pulls back so you can see your character performing the action from a distance.

Rule of thumb: if the camera represents the character’s own direct line of sight, it is first-person. If the camera is outside the character and shows their body, it is third-person.
